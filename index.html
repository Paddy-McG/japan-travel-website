Hokkaido

Hokkaido is the furtest north region of japan. It is home to a single prefecture also called Hokkaido. As the region is the most northern, it is often covered in thick layers of snow through most of the year. The snow provides a beautiful landscape for photography and amazing spot for practicing snowsports and then relaxing in a nice hot spring. Hokkaidos railway system is lacking compare to the rest of the regions, JR does connect all the major citys and towns but not the smaller ones. If you want to access the more rural areas a bus or a rental car is recommended.

Tohoku

Home to 6 prefectures called Aomori, Iwata, Akita, Yamagata, Miyagi and Fukushima. Sendai is the biggest of all the cities in Tohoku. Sendai is easily accesiable by a bullet train from Tokyo. Aomori is one of the snowiest places in the worldduring the Winter. During the Summer there is a huge festivalwith large floats. Iwata has the lowwest density population of all the prefectures on Honshu so it's the perfect place to explore off the beaten path. Enjoy the beautiful food and the hop in a relaxing onsen bath in the evening, enjoy the day out and go say hello to the akita, which is native to the prefecture. Yumugata is famous for its plethra of onsesens and natural hot springs. You can head up a scenic route that leads to a beutiful lookout, Yamadera. Miyagi, home of Sendai, the city of many delicious and spectacular cuisine. Funaoka, a city just outside of Sendai is home to beautiful sakura trees and an amazing cable car that lets you enjoy them from above. Fukushima famous for the terrible nuclear disaster but has not fully recovered since then, none the less it is still a beautiful place with many destinstions to enjoy.

Kanto

Home to 7 of the prefectures, Ibaraki, Tochigi, Chiba, Gunma, Saitama, Tokyo & Kanagawa prefecture. Ibaraki has numerous beautiful shrines throught the prefecture. Visit the beatiful Kairakuen Garden, knowen for being one of the prettiest gardens in the whole of Japan. Head into tochigi visiting Utsunomiya City and enjoy their famous gyozo dumplings. Pay a visit to Nikko and enjoy each of the beautiful shrines in your own time, the shrines and the path leading to them look magical under the colourful leaves of nearby trees in the Autumn. Chiba lies just to the East of Tokyo well worth a day trip at least. Visit one od the most instagramable waterfalls/caves in japan, head to mother farm to enjoy the beautiful and friendly animals they have there. Visit the Disney Parks and enjoy the fun. Visit Mt. Nokogiri home of a huge Buddah carved into the mountain face. Gunma, the prefecture of many beautiful hotsprings decorated about, the most famous ones being kusatsu onsen, manza onsen and Shima onsen. Kusatsu is the most famous of all the springs in Japan and is a must go to if you are in the area. Dont be afraid to take a visit outside the onsens and head to Oze National Park, a beautiful trail taking in nature around you. On your trip to Saitama visit the small town of Gyoda, a town full of historical knowledge just like most of Saitama, full of history. Visit the Arakawa River and enjoy a relaxing boat ride through the scenic banks. Tokyo the capital city of Japan and the most famous City of them all. You could spend days and days exploring this huge metropilis and still not explored anywhere close to half of the little suprises it has in store. Famously the Tokyo tower, shibuya, Teamlabs Planet and The Tokyo National Museum or visit the numerous different shrines. Visit the city of kamakura in Kanagawa prefecture, home to a huge Buddah statue and lots of historical information. Go to Hakone and see a beautiful view of Mt.Fuji across Lake Ashinoko. Finally visit Japans second biggest city, Yokohama.
